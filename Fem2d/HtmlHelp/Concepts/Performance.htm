Performance

The Fem2d engine is designed to be a lightweight, efficient component for both script-based clients and vtable-based clients. However, there are some things that you should think about to get the best performance. Here are some tips:

Joint Numbering

The engine uses a Banded-Cholesky based solver. It also does not optimize bandwidth prior to solution. This means that it is up to you, the client programmer, to try and create models with the least amount of bandwidth possible. The basic goal is to try to minimize the maximum difference between joint numbers (ID's) across elements in the model. Joint numbers matter, element numbers do not. Use POI's Instead of Joints for Capturing Results

There are two ways to get force and displacement results data from a Model: You can place Joint's at the desired locations, or you can place POI's. As shown in the previous example, whenever you place a Joint in the model you increase the bandwidth of the system matrix and slow performance. It is much faster to obtain results at POI's than it is to obtain them at Joints. The only exception to this rule is if you already have a Joint at the location for another reason.
