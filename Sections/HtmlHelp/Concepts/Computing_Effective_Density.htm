Computing Effective Density

Typical finite element programs will compute the self-weight dead load of beam elements. To do so they multiply the cross sectional area of the beam with its unit weight (density times gravitational acceleration).

The cross sectional area input into finite element programs is the structural area from which the axial stiffness EA is computed. This area is typically less than the actual area of the beam. Unless an effective density is used, the self-weight dead load will be underestimated.

The effective density times the structual area will provide the same weight as the actual density times the actual area. To compute the effective density for input to a finite element program, divide the MassPerLength property of a MassProperties object by the structural area.
