Computing the First Moment of Area

The first moment of area is an important property when computing shear stresses. Because the first moment of area differs for each location of a section, the ElasticProperties object (and the ShapeProperties object from the WBFL Geometry Library) do not compute it.

Use the following procedure to compute the first moment of area.

  1. Get the ElasticProperties of your section.

  2. At the location where you want the first moment of area computed, create a line object

  3. Use the line object to clip your section. This will create a new section that consists of only the portion of the original section that was above the clipping line

  4. Get the ElasticProperties of the clipped section

  5. The first moment of area is the area of the clipped section times the distance between the centroids of the original section and the clipped section
