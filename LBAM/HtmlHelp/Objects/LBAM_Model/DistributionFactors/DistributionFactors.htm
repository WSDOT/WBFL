DistributionFactors

Collection describing the layout of distribution factors along the superstructure. The start of the layout is at the left support or the left end of the left overhang, if present.

Distribution factors are laid out in prismatic segments (DistributionFactorSegment objects) along the entire length of the superstructure. The actual values of live load distribution factors are contained in the DistributionFactor objects that are contained in this collection.

Tolerancing of Layout

In an ideal world, the total length of the DistributionFactorSegments would be exactly equal to the total length of the superstructure. However, this is the real world, and we are dealing with humans, computers, and imperfect floating point numbers. Hence, it is both impractical and inconvenient to try to enforce this restriction. This means that we must "fudge" the length of the collection of segmetns in order to make it always fit perfectly within the superstructure length. The following rules are used to resolve this:

Superstructure is Symmetrical

If the IsSymmetrical property of the collection is true, then the layout of segments across the superstructure is forced to be symmetrical about the centerline (midpoint) of the superstructure. For this case, it would be ideal if the length of the collection was exactly equal to one-half of the length of the superstructure. Again, enforcing perfection is not practical, and misfits are resolved in the same manner as for nonsymmetrical members - except now segments are extended or truncated to the mid-point of the superstructure. Determining the Actual Layout

As you can see from the above discussion, the actual layout of DistributionFactorSegments can be adjusted from the input data under certain conditions. The methods GetSegmentForMemberLocation, and GetMemberSegments can be used to compute and retrieve the actual layout as computed by the LBAM.
