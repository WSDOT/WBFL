StressPoint

Represents a stress calculation location on a CrossSection.

Note that, if you are a civil engineer, this is not the way you are accustomed to calculating stresses. First of all, there is no location data associated with this stress point - all information is contained within the stress coeffiicients. This may seem a bit strange at first, but it makes stress calculation general - especially for composite sections.

In the LBAM, stresses at a cross section location are calculated by:

 

s = Fx * Sa + M * Sm

Where:

 

 

 

s =

The axial stress at the stress point location

 

Sa, Sm

Are the stress coefficients as defined below

 

Fx =

The Axial force at the section (tension is positive)

 

M =

The moment at the section (positive moment makes the beam smile)

Note that StressPoint objects are contained in the StressPoints collection, which behaves like a vector. And, that for multi-stage problems, stresses must be summed over Stages. This means that the number of stress points at a given location along a member (Support, TemporarySupport, SuperstructureMember) must always be the same throughout all defined Stages.

Properties

Sa

Stress coefficient for axial force = 1/Area

Sm

Stress coefficient for moment = 1/S

Clone

Creates a clone of this StressPoint
