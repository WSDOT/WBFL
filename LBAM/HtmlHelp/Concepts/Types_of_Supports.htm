Types of Supports

Support and TemporarySupport members come in two flavors: Idealized (zero-length), and Fixed-Length. From the aspect of input, the only difference between a zero-length Support and a fixed-length Support is the value of the Support's Length property. If the value is 0.0, it is a zero-length support. However, the two types of supports are modeled slightly differently as explained below.

Idealized (Zero-Length) Support Members

If a Support has its Length property equal to zero, it is called a zero-length, or idealized Support. A Zero Length Support simply defines the idealized boundary conditions at span ends. A Zero Length Support has zero size and is coincident with its bearing end.

The Y Translational degree of freedom must be fixed.

Both the X translation and Z rotational degrees of freedom can be fixed or freed independently (the suport can be fixed, pinned, or roller - remember Statics 101?)

Fixed-Length Support Members

Fixed-Length Support Members are defined by having their Length property greater than zero.

Fixed-Length Support Members are straight, vertically-oriented beams that connect at the top to Span Members.

Fixed-Length Support Members have two ends, a Bearing End, and a Footing End. The Bearing End connects to the Span Members' end and is always located above the Footing End.

All degrees of freedom at the Footing End are fixed.

The geometry of a Fixed-Length Support Member is defined by its length, and horizontal inclination.

The section properties of a Fixed-Length Support are defined by prismatic segments along its length.

Fixed-Length Support Members may be inclined from vertical either upstation or downstation. Inclination is specified by horizontal offset.
