Live Loads

The LBAM includes a very flexible model for describing live loads. The model was designed such that all live loads in the LRFD and LFD specifications can be described. User-defined permit and pedestrian live loads are also supported. The object model for LiveLoad is shown below.

Although live load results can be viewed separately, they are generally used by designers as part of a LoadCombination. Combinations can include the responses of one of six different LiveLoadModels: Deflection, Design, Fatigue, Pedestrian, Permit, and Special. The data for each LiveLoadModel is arbitrary and is defined by the user.

LiveLoadModel

A LiveLoadModel can be thought of as a collection of Truck/Lane combinations called VehicularLoads. The results for the VehicularLoads are then enveloped to create the LiveLoadModel results. For example: in LRFD the HL93 vehicular load results are computed by enveloping results of the design truck+lane, design tandem+lane, etc... Another example is the LFD vehicular live load, which is computed by enveloping the results of the variable axle design truck with the single axle rider and lane envelope.

VehicularLoad

A VehicularLoad is a combination of a multi-axled truck and a lane load. The truck can have a variable axle spacing. The lane and truck results can be combined in several different ways.
