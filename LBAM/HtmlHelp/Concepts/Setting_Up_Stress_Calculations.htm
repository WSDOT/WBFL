Setting Up Stress Calculations

If a populated StressPoints collection exists at a POI, an LBAM analysis engine can compute stresses at that location.

Stresses are computed using the stress coefficients, Sa and Sm, at a StressPoint along with the member forces at the location using the formula:

 

s = P*Sa + M*Sm

Where:

  1. P = Axial force at the location (tension is positive)

  2. M = Moment at the location

At first, this form might seem a bit different to some users (think Sa=1/A, and Sm=c/I or 1/S), but it provides a flexible and expedient way to input data into the LBAM.

Specifying StressPoints

There are two ways to specify StressPoints at a POI location.

1) Explicitly populate the StressPoints collection at the POI object in question. Or,

2) Populate the StressPoints collection at the CrossSection located at the POI.

The first method provides a way to override any StressPoints that might be at the cross section. This can be handy when modeling haunched girders where a prismatic section model might be good enough for modeling stiffness properties, but may not provide adequate accuracy for stress calculations.

Accumulation of Stresses Over Stages

Stress results can also be accumulated over Stages by the LBAM engines, if requested. However, the number of StressPoint's at a location must be constant over all stages (e.g., if there are four StressPoints in "Stage1", there must be four StressPoints in "Stage2" and "Stage3"). If this is not the case, an analysis error will occur.
