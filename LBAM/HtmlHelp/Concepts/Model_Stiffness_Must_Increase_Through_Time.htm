Model Stiffness Must Increase Through Time

The stiffnesses of all structural properties at a given location in the model must remain equal or increase through time.

The analysis engines in the LBAM perform stage-dependent analyses by summing results sequentially over each defined stage. A reduction in stiffness properties at a latter stage would cause loadings from previous stages to affect the latter stage. This type of behavior is not correctly modelled by the analysis engines in the LBAM.

An exception to this rule is the removal of TemporarySupports. Weakening of the system caused by removal of temporary supports is accounted for correctly.

NOTE: Reductions in overall system stiffness caused by weakening of members over time are not checked by the analysis engines. And, this type of input will result in incorrect answers for the analysis. It is up to the model builder (you) to insure that stiffnesses increase over stages.
