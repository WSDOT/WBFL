Member Loads

Member Loads are loads that are applied directly to parts of the LBAM model. Every Member Load must be assigned to a LoadGroup. The following paragraphs give a brief discussion of each type of member load that is available. More detailed descriptions of each load type are provided in the API documentation.

DistributedLoad

A DistributedLoad can be applied along a Span, SuperStructureMember, Support, or TemporarySupport. A DistributedLoad has a linear load distribution and can be applied anywhere along the length of a member. A DistrubutedLoad may not be applied to zero-length members. A DistributedLoad can be applied in member local, or global coordinates.

PointLoad

A PointLoad is a discrete force/moment that can be applied along a Span, SuperStructureMember, Support, or TemporarySupport. A PointLoad can be applied in member local, or global coordinates.

SettlementLoad

A SettlementLoad is a specified translation at the foundation end of a Support or TemporarySupport.

StrainLoad

A StrainLoad allows the specification of a pre-existing, uniform axial or curvature strain along the entire length of a member. A StrainLoad can be applied along a Span, SuperStructureMember, Support, or TemporarySupport. Pre-existing strains are used to model fabrication errors or material shrinkage.

TemperatureLoad

A TemperatureLoad provides a way to specify a strain due to a uniform temperature change along a member. The temperature change can be full-depth, or a linear gradient. Strains due to a TemperatureLoad are computed using the ThermalCoeff, and Depth properties of the cross section at the load location. A TemperatureLoad can be applied along a Span, SuperStructureMember, Support, or TemporarySupport.
